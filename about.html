About Me

My journey into the world of GIS began during my Bachelor's program in Land and Water Resources Management, where I first discovered the incredible potential of geospatial technologies. This initial exposure ignited my passion for GIS and inspired me to delve deeper into the field, ultimately shaping my professional path as a GIS Specialist.

My GIS interests include spatial planning, network analysis, data visualization, and the use of open-source tools, among others. I appreciate the opportunities to explore innovative approaches to a range of problems, such as sustainable development and enhancing the overall quality of life. I firmly believe in the power of collaboration and innovation to address intricate urban issues and shape better cities for the future.

Outside of work, I enjoy hiking and playing board games with friends and family, where I occasionally unleash my competitive side.

Skills Inventory

Programming: Developed Ottawalk, a personalized itinerary mapping application for Ottawa, leveraging open-source tools and data (Javascript, Leaflet, Geoserver, PostgreSQL, OSM).

Data Analysis and Predictive Modelling: Conducted an exploratory analysis on a BoardGameGeek.com database and built a machine learning model to predict game difficulty (R, Markdown, Tidyr, dplyr, ggplot, rpart)

Geospatial Data Analysis: Analyzed Ottawa's downtown walking street network using various libraries, visualized data layers, and calculated network indicators (pandas, geopandas, osmnx, networkx, matplotlib, shapely).

Geographic Information Systems (GIS): Implemented a GIS system for Hiboo Networks using ESRI products, facilitating improved network analysis and visualizations.

Cartography: Produced a forest types characteristics map using ArcGIS Pro as part of the Geographic Information Systems Certificate Program at NSCC, Lawrencetown, Nova Scotia.
